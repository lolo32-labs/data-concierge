## Naming Strategy

### Why We Need to Rename

The current name "DataConcierge" has three problems:

1. **Too generic.** "Data Concierge" could be anything -- a BI tool, a consulting firm, a database product. It doesn't communicate the specific value proposition (profit tracking for Shopify merchants).
2. **No distribution signal.** Shopify App Store search rewards names that contain relevant keywords. "DataConcierge" won't surface for searches like "profit tracking" or "profit analytics."
3. **Wrong mental model.** "Concierge" implies a luxury service or a generic assistant. We want to signal focused profit expertise, not open-ended helpfulness.

### Naming Criteria

The new name must:
- Contain "profit" (for App Store search and immediate value recognition)
- Be available as a .com or .io domain
- Not conflict with existing Shopify App Store listings
- Have available social handles (@name on Twitter/X, Instagram)
- Be easy to say, spell, and remember
- Be 2 words max (shorter is better for App Store title constraints)

## Competitive Naming Landscape

### How Profit-Adjacent Apps Name Themselves

| App Name | Naming Pattern | Reviews | Our Takeaway |
|----------|---------------|---------|-------------|
| TrueProfit | [Adjective] + Profit | 540+ | "True" implies accuracy. They own this positioning. Don't compete on "true/accurate" naming. |
| BeProfit | [Verb] + Profit | 300+ | Action-oriented. "Be" is weak though. |
| OrderMetrics | [Object] + Metrics | 200+ | Descriptive but not memorable. Sounds enterprise. |
| Lifetimely | Made-up word | 400+ | Works because it's unique, but doesn't contain "profit" keyword. |
| Triple Whale | Unrelated brand name | 1000+ | They can afford a non-descriptive name because of their marketing budget. We can't. |

### Naming Insight

For a bootstrapped app with zero brand awareness, **a descriptive name beats a creative name.** We need the Shopify App Store to surface us when merchants search "profit." Once we have 100+ reviews and brand recognition, the name matters less.

## Shopify App Store Title

### Title Constraints

- **Maximum 30 characters** for the app name
- Best practice: [Brand Name] - [Key Benefit]
- Keywords in the title significantly impact search ranking

### Recommended App Store Title

> **ProfitSight - Real Profit in Seconds**

(30 characters exactly, including spaces)

### App Store Listing Elements

| Element | Content | Constraints |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **App name** | ProfitSight - Real Profit in Seconds | 30 chars max |
| **App introduction** | Know your real Shopify profit instantly. Ask questions, get answers -- no spreadsheet needed. | 100 chars |
| **App details** | Connect your Shopify store and ask: "What's my real profit?" Get instant answers about product margins, ad ROI, channel profitability, and more. We calculate the numbers Shopify doesn't -- COGS, fees, returns, shipping -- so you see what you actually keep. | 500 chars |
| **Category** | Analytics | -- |
| **Keywords** | profit tracking, profit analytics, COGS tracking, profit calculator, net profit | 5 keywords |

## Brand Voice

### Tone

| Attribute | How We Sound | How We Don't Sound |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Directness | "Your real profit is $8,247" | "Based on our analysis, your estimated..." |
| Simplicity | "Ask a question, get an answer" | "Leverage our AI-powered analytics platform" |
| Confidence | "We calculate what Shopify can't" | "We try to approximate your profit" |
| Honesty | "This estimate doesn't include 5 products" | Hiding uncertainty |
| Empathy | "You shouldn't need a spreadsheet for this" | "Our proprietary algorithm" |

### Key Phrases

- "Know your real profit without the spreadsheet"
- "Ask questions, get answers"
- "The profit number Shopify doesn't show you"
- "Connect your store, see your profit in 3 minutes"
- "$19/mo -- about the cost of one Shopify app"

### What We Never Say

- "AI-powered" (in customer-facing copy) -- merchants don't care about AI, they care about answers
- "Dashboard" -- we're not a dashboard product
- "Analytics platform" -- too enterprise, too vague
- "Big data" or "machine learning" -- irrelevant to our audience
